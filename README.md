#### Abstract<br>
People with HIV (PWH) often endure comorbid mental health challenges, disproportionately rooted in violent trauma, with maladaptive coping (e.g. hazardous alcohol use) undermining treatment adherence. Momentary stressors can be both intra-individual and environmental: typically place-based. Evidence-based interventions such as _Living in the Face of Trauma_ (LIFT) impart adaptive coping skills, but lack scalability, context awareness, and real-time accessibility.
